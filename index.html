The weekly shape

Five sessions, five different jobs. Each one is 25 minutes — the structure is what makes short sessions add up. Weekends are for rest, or for playing purely for pleasure with no agenda.

House rules

  • Practice slowly enough to play correctly. Speed is a by-product of accuracy, never the other way around.
  • Loop small: two to four bars at a time. Playing a piece from the top every time only polishes the first page.
  • Metronome on for technique work, off for musical shaping. Both matter.
  • When sight-reading, never stop and never go back. Keeping the pulse is the whole exercise.
  • Record yourself every Friday. The phone microphone is an honest teacher.
  • Sheet music for nearly all the classical repertoire here is free on IMSLP; licensed arrangements of the film and game pieces are on Musicnotes or Sheet Music Plus.

Repertoire path

Tap a piece to cycle its status. Learn three to four pieces per level to a genuinely finished state — plus the milestone — before moving up. Timelines are honest estimates at five short sessions a week; moving slower than the label is normal, not failure.
